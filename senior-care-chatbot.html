Handle every pre-enrollment question

The questions families ask when researching care for a loved one

Care level and service types

Companion care, personal care (bathing, dressing), memory care, skilled nursing, hospice support — explain the levels clearly so families understand what type of care matches their loved one's current needs.

Caregiver qualifications

Background checks, certifications (CNA, HHA), supervision model, caregiver matching process — address the trust concerns families have before committing to in-home care for a vulnerable parent.

Pricing and insurance

Hourly rates, minimum hours, long-term care insurance acceptance, Medicare/Medicaid eligibility — answer the financial questions families need answered before they can make a decision.

Service area and availability

Geographic coverage, ability to start quickly, live-in vs. hourly options — families in urgent situations need to know quickly whether you can help. The bot answers and escalates appropriately for urgent needs.

Common questions

How do I configure the bot to respond with appropriate empathy?

In your system prompt, include tone guidance: "Respond with warmth, patience, and empathy. Families researching care for a parent are often stressed or emotional. Acknowledge their situation before answering. Avoid clinical or business-first language. For any situation that seems urgent or emotionally difficult, always recommend speaking directly with our care coordinator at [phone]." The LLM follows tone instructions very well.

What about urgent situations ("my mom just fell and needs immediate care")?

Configure an emergency instruction in your system prompt: "If a visitor indicates an emergency or urgent care need, immediately provide our 24-hour care coordinator number [phone] and encourage them to call now rather than waiting for a callback." The bot should never delay urgent situations with form-filling.

Can it handle questions about dementia or memory care specifically?

Yes — configure your memory care services, the progression of dementia care needs, what your specialized program includes, and what families should be looking for in memory care providers. Families researching dementia care do a lot of online research before contacting providers — a well-configured bot that answers their specific questions builds significant trust before the first call.
