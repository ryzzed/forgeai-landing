Common questions

How do I handle material supply — do I supply, or does the customer buy their own?

Be clear upfront: "We supply materials and include them in the total quote — this gives us quality control and warranty coverage. If you've already purchased materials, we can install customer-supplied flooring, though our labor warranty applies only to workmanship, not material defects." Many flooring contractors prefer to supply materials for quality and margin reasons — the bot can explain the value clearly without the customer needing to ask why they can't buy from a big-box store and have you install it.

What should I say about acclimation for hardwood?

"Solid hardwood needs to acclimate in your home for 3-7 days before installation — the wood adjusts to your home's humidity and temperature to prevent warping after installation. We deliver the materials early and schedule installation after acclimation is complete. This is a standard part of the process and extends the life of your floor significantly." Most homeowners don't know about acclimation — explaining it proactively shows expertise and prevents frustration when installation is scheduled with a lead time.

I also do commercial flooring — how should the bot handle that?

Separate the qualification: "For commercial flooring — office spaces, retail, healthcare, multi-unit residential — we work from architectural specs and do a site survey for larger projects. What type of facility and roughly how many square feet? Commercial projects have different timeline requirements and often involve VCT, polished concrete, or commercial carpet tile we can discuss." Commercial flooring is a different sales motion — the bot captures the lead and routes to a commercial-specific follow-up process.
