What is the difference between fence stain, sealer, and paint?
Penetrating stain soaks into wood fibers, providing UV and moisture protection while maintaining a natural wood appearance — the preferred option for cedar and redwood. Film-forming sealers sit on top and provide waterproofing. Solid paint provides full color coverage but requires stripping when it peels. ChatFlow recommends the right product based on wood type and desired appearance.
How often should a wood fence be stained or sealed?
Most wood fences in moderate climates need re-staining every 3-5 years. Fences in harsh sun, coastal salt air, or heavy rain climates may need attention every 2-3 years. ChatFlow asks about fence age, last treatment date, and visible symptoms to recommend the correct service and urgency level.
