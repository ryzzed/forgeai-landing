Common questions

Can the chatbot help homeowners understand if their foundation crack is serious?

You configure the chatbot to explain crack severity indicators in plain language — hairline vertical cracks are common settlement and usually cosmetic; horizontal cracks in block foundations signal lateral pressure and need immediate evaluation; stair-step cracks in brick may indicate differential settlement. The chatbot always concludes that a free inspection is needed for an accurate assessment — building trust without practicing engineering.

How does the chatbot explain foundation repair costs?

Foundation repair spans a wide range depending on method and severity. Configure typical ranges in the system prompt: crack injection (epoxy or polyurethane) $300–800 per crack; interior drainage system $8,000–15,000 for an average basement; push pier or helical pier systems $1,200–2,000 per pier with average jobs requiring 8–12 piers. The chatbot frames these as ranges that a free inspection will narrow down significantly, which sets expectations without sticker-shocking the customer.

How does the chatbot handle the waterproofing vs drainage vs structural repair question?

Foundation companies often offer multiple services that customers confuse. You configure the chatbot to distinguish: waterproofing (preventing water intrusion from outside), interior drainage (managing water that gets in), and structural repair (stabilizing movement or settling). Customers describe their symptom — wet basement, visible crack, sticking doors, sloping floors — and the chatbot explains which category likely applies and why an inspection is needed to confirm the right solution.
