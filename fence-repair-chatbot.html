Common questions

How much does fence repair cost?

Single post replacement runs $150–300. Gate repair runs $100–250. Section replacement runs $200–450 per section. Storm damage repairs covering multiple sections run $500–1,500. Full replacement for 150–200 linear feet of 6-ft privacy fence runs $3,000–7,000 for wood. The chatbot asks about damage type and extent to give a relevant range before the first call.

How does the chatbot handle the repair vs replace decision?

Configure the chatbot to ask fence age, material, number of damaged sections, and whether posts are rotted. It explains when repair makes sense (isolated damage, posts solid, fence under 10 years) vs when replacement is more economical (posts failing, widespread damage, fence over 15 years). Honest guidance builds trust and converts customers even when the answer is "repair is fine for now."

How does the chatbot handle insurance claim situations?

Configure the chatbot to ask whether damage was storm-related and whether the customer plans to file a homeowners insurance claim. If so, route for priority assessment with insurance documentation support. Insurance-funded repairs convert at high rates — customers who find a company that handles documentation are more likely to stay with that company for the full project.
