What the chatbot handles for your solar company

Pre-educate prospects and filter out unqualified leads before they reach your reps

System sizing and cost estimates

Ask about monthly electric bill and service address to give ballpark system size and cost ranges. Prospects who understand the investment before calling are more serious — and more likely to close.

Incentives and financing

Explain the 30% federal tax credit, state incentives, utility rebates, and financing options (purchase, loan, lease, PPA). Demystifying the financial side removes the biggest barrier between interest and action.

Roof and home qualification

Ask about roof age, orientation, shading, and ownership — the factors that determine whether solar makes sense. Route clearly unqualified homes (north-facing, heavily shaded, rental) before they consume your reps' time.

Site assessment capture

When a prospect is qualified and ready, capture their name, address, contact info, electric bill, and preferred contact time — everything your rep needs to prepare a targeted proposal before the first conversation.

Common questions

Can the chatbot handle battery storage questions too?

Yes — configure your battery storage offerings (Tesla Powerwall, Enphase IQ Battery, Franklin WH, etc.) alongside your solar panels. Battery questions are common: 'Will I have power during an outage?' and 'Does battery storage improve my ROI?' are frequently asked. If you offer storage as an add-on, the chatbot can explain the cost, backup capacity, and value proposition — and note it in the lead capture form so your rep can address it specifically.

We serve multiple states with different incentives — how do I handle that?

Configure the chatbot to ask for the prospect's state or zip code early in the conversation, then provide state-specific incentive information. 'In California, you may also qualify for the SGIP battery rebate' is more useful than generic information. For complex multi-state incentive structures, the bot can give general federal credit info and note that a site assessment will include a full local incentive analysis.

Can I use the chatbot for commercial solar leads too?

Yes — configure separate sections for residential and commercial solar. Commercial prospects have different questions: utility rate structures, demand charges, ITC vs. bonus depreciation, and longer decision cycles. Many solar companies configure the bot to route commercial inquiries to a dedicated commercial sales contact, since the sales process differs significantly from residential.
