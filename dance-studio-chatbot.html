The questions parents ask before they sign up their child

Style and class matching

Ballet, hip hop, jazz, lyrical, tap, contemporary, ballroom — match students to the right style based on their age, interests, and experience level. Help parents choose without needing a phone call.

Age and level placement

Mini dancers (3-5), beginner, intermediate, teen, adult — explain your class structure so parents know exactly where their child fits before they enroll. Prevents misplacement and drop-offs.

Tuition and recital costs

Monthly tuition, registration fees, costume costs, recital tickets — the full picture of what dance actually costs. Parents who get clear answers upfront are more likely to commit and less likely to churn.

Trial class capture

When a parent is ready to try, capture their name, child's age, preferred day, and email — giving you everything needed to confirm the trial class without a back-and-forth phone call.

Common questions

Can the chatbot handle adult class questions too?

Yes — configure adult class schedules, styles (adult ballet, salsa, Zumba, adult hip hop), and pricing separately in your system prompt. Adult students asking late at night whether there's a beginners class they can join without feeling out of place get an immediate answer instead of waiting for a callback.

What about competitive team vs. recreational questions?

If you have both a recreational program and a competitive/company team, configure the distinction clearly: different commitment levels, audition requirements, additional fees (competition entry, costumes, travel), and time expectations. This prevents mismatched expectations and saves hours of conversation with families who aren't prepared for the competitive commitment.

Can it handle drop-in class questions vs. enrolled classes?

Yes — if you offer drop-in pricing for certain classes alongside monthly enrollment, configure both options in the system prompt. The bot can explain the difference, help visitors decide which makes sense for their situation, and capture the appropriate type of inquiry for each.
