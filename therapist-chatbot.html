Questions therapists ask us

Can a chatbot handle sensitive mental health inquiries appropriately?

Yes — you define exactly what the bot covers. It handles practice information (services, insurance, scheduling, what to expect) and directs any clinical or crisis questions to call your office or 988. It never provides diagnoses or clinical advice.

What happens if someone mentions a crisis or suicidal thoughts?

ChatFlow is configured to detect crisis language and immediately respond with crisis resources — 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, 911, and your office number. It does not attempt to handle emergency situations via chat. You can review and customize this response.

Can it answer insurance and billing questions?

Yes. Train it with the insurance panels you accept, your self-pay rates, sliding scale policy, and superbill information. It handles the routine billing questions that otherwise fill your voicemail.

Is conversation data private and HIPAA-conscious?

ChatFlow is designed for general inquiries — services, scheduling, billing — not for the collection of clinical information. We recommend not using it to collect PHI (protected health information). Conversations are stored securely and only accessible to you.

Does it work with SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, or other EHR systems?

ChatFlow works as a front-door intake tool on your website. It can direct patients to your scheduling link (Calendly, SimplePractice Client Portal, etc.) — it doesn't replace your EHR, it feeds leads into it.
