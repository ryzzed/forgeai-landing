What the chatbot handles for your inspection business

Booking intake and scoping

Property size, type (single family, condo, multi-unit), age, and any specific concerns (foundation, roof, water intrusion). Captures the information you need to schedule and estimate duration — before you ever pick up the phone.

Pricing by size and add-ons

Standard inspection rates by square footage, plus add-on services: radon, mold, sewer scope, pool/spa, infrared thermal imaging, well and septic. Buyers comparing inspectors get clear pricing instead of "call for a quote."

What's included in the inspection

Systems covered (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, roof, foundation, windows), report format and turnaround time, whether buyers can attend. First-time buyers especially need this explained — the bot answers it without you having to walk through it on every intake call.

Credentials and coverage

State license, certifications (InterNACHI, ASHI), years of experience, E&O insurance coverage. Home buyers want to know who's walking through the biggest purchase of their life — the bot establishes credibility before the first conversation.

Common questions

How do I handle real estate agent referrals through the chatbot?

When a buyer mentions their agent sent them, capture both: "Great — can I ask who referred you? We work with a lot of local agents and want to make sure we're taking good care of their clients." This tells you your referral source and creates a moment to thank the agent later. You can also configure the bot to mention: "We're happy to coordinate directly with your agent's office for scheduling if that's easier." Agents appreciate inspectors who make their transaction smoother.

Can the chatbot explain what a pre-listing inspection is?

Yes — this is a growth opportunity worth explaining: "A pre-listing inspection is done before you put your home on the market. You get the same thorough report a buyer would get — so you can address issues proactively, price accurately, and avoid surprises that kill deals in escrow. Many sellers find it reduces negotiation and speeds up the closing." Sellers searching for pre-listing inspections are an underserved segment for many inspectors — configure the bot to mention this service proactively when someone asks about selling a home.

What should I say about limitations of the inspection?

Be matter-of-fact: "A home inspection is a visual, non-invasive examination — we inspect what's accessible and visible. We're not able to see inside walls, under concrete slabs, or into sealed areas. We report on the condition of the home at the time of inspection." This sets accurate expectations before the inspection and protects you from post-closing complaints about things that weren't observable. Saying it upfront in chat is better than explaining it after a buyer is upset.
