Common questions

How often does a chimney need to be swept?

The NFPA recommends annual inspection for all fireplaces. Cleaning frequency depends on use — wood-burning fireplaces used regularly (2+ cords per season) need annual sweeping; occasional use may go 2–3 years between sweeps, but annual inspection is still recommended. Gas fireplaces need annual inspection but less frequent cleaning. The chatbot explains the guidance and books the appointment before the customer decides to wait another year.

What does chimney sweep pricing typically include?

You configure your service pricing in the system prompt. A standard chimney sweep + Level 1 inspection runs $150–250 for most residential fireplaces. Level 2 inspection (required for home sale or after a fire event) adds $100–200 for camera inspection. Chimney cap installation $200–450. Firebox repointing or crown repair $300–800 depending on extent. The chatbot gives these ranges and books the appointment.

How does the chatbot handle customers worried about creosote buildup or chimney fires?

Creosote and chimney fire concerns are high-urgency. Configure the chatbot to explain that heavy glazed creosote buildup (Stage 3) is a fire hazard requiring professional removal, and that any chimney fire — even a small incident — requires a Level 2 inspection before the fireplace can be used again. The chatbot validates the concern, explains the risk clearly, and books a priority inspection appointment.
