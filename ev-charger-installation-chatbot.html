New EV owner urgency capture

EV owners are most motivated to install a Level 2 charger in the first 2–4 weeks of ownership — the Level 1 charging experience is frustrating enough to drive immediate action, but that urgency fades as they adapt. Configure ChatFlow to ask about the vehicle and current charging setup: if they're using a 120V wall outlet on a new EV, that's maximum urgency. Responding immediately with a real price and a booking path converts these customers before competitors call back or the initial frustration fades.

Panel upgrade pre-qualification

The panel upgrade question is the single biggest source of EV charger quote surprise — configure ChatFlow to ask about panel amperage upfront and explain what triggers an upgrade. 200A panel: rarely needs an upgrade for one EV. 100A panel: may need an upgrade if already loaded with HVAC, electric water heater, or other high-draw appliances — or a load management device ($300–600) as a lower-cost alternative. Pre-surfacing this turns a sticker-shock objection at estimate time into an expected and explained option.

Federal tax credit and utility rebate capture

The federal Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit covers 30% of Level 2 charger installation cost up to $1,000 for homeowners — configure ChatFlow to mention this when quoting. Many utilities also offer EV charger rebates ($100–500) and time-of-use rate programs. Customers who learn about rebates from your chatbot remember you as the contractor who helped them understand their full savings picture — which is a stronger close than the contractor who gave the lowest number without context.

Multi-EV and future-proofing upsell

Households with one EV frequently add a second within 2–3 years — configure ChatFlow to ask whether the customer anticipates a second vehicle and mention that running a second conduit during the initial installation adds only $50–150 vs $300–500 to pull separately later. Future-proofing is a natural upsell that customers appreciate because it saves them money long-term. This also creates the future service call when the second EV arrives and they need the second circuit activated.

Common questions

How does the chatbot quote EV charger installation?

You configure price ranges by complexity. Standard Level 2 install (40–50 amp circuit, short run): $400–800 labor. Long cable run (50+ feet): $600–1,200. Panel upgrade (100A to 200A): $1,500–3,500 additional. Smart charger supply and install: $600–1,500 including hardware. The chatbot asks about panel amperage, garage distance, and charger preference, gives a combined range, and books the free electrical assessment.

How does the chatbot explain Level 1 vs Level 2 charging?

Level 1 (120V): adds 3–5 miles per hour — fine for plug-in hybrids, inadequate for most EVs. Level 2 (240V dedicated circuit): adds 15–30 miles per hour — full charge overnight in 6–12 hours. The chatbot asks about daily mileage and vehicle type to confirm whether Level 2 is essential or just convenient, then books the assessment.

How does the chatbot handle panel upgrade questions?

The chatbot asks about current panel amperage (100A vs 200A) and high-draw appliances to flag potential upgrade needs. Panel upgrade: $1,500–3,500. Load management device (avoids full upgrade): $300–600. Pre-surfacing this during the chat prevents estimate-day sticker shock and keeps customers from feeling upsold at the visit.
