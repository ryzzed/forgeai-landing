Why website chat captures different (often better) leads

Facebook Messenger bots and website chatbots reach fundamentally different audiences

Google visitors have higher intent

Someone who searched "plumber near me" and landed on your website is actively looking to hire. Someone who messaged you on Facebook may just be curious. Website chatbot leads tend to convert at higher rates because the visitor already expressed purchase intent.

No follower base required

Chatfuel works for businesses with an established Facebook or Instagram following — the bot responds to people who already follow you. ChatFlow works for any business with a website, regardless of social media presence. It captures people finding you for the first time.

Works for service businesses without social strategy

Many local service businesses (contractors, clinics, legal, B2B) get the majority of their leads from Google, not Instagram. A website chatbot captures that traffic directly. A Messenger bot reaches only the portion of their audience on social media.

SEO and paid search compatible

If you're running Google Ads or investing in SEO, every visitor lands on your website — not your Facebook page. ChatFlow captures the leads from that investment. Chatfuel only captures leads who message you through Meta's platforms.

Common questions

Should I use Chatfuel and ChatFlow together?

They're complementary, not competing. Chatfuel handles your Facebook Messenger and Instagram DM traffic — automating responses to social followers who message your business page. ChatFlow handles your website visitors — capturing leads from people who find you on Google or visit your site directly. If you're active on both channels, using both makes sense. If you primarily get leads from your website (most service businesses), ChatFlow is the higher-value tool.

Can ChatFlow work on Facebook or Instagram too?

ChatFlow is designed to be embedded on websites as a chat widget. It doesn't integrate with Facebook Messenger or Instagram DMs — those are separate channels that require Meta's platform-specific APIs. ChatFlow is the right tool for website visitors; Chatfuel or ManyChat are the right tools for Meta platform users.

My business gets most leads from Instagram — should I still use a website chatbot?

If your primary lead channel is Instagram, focus your chatbot investment there — Chatfuel or ManyChat are appropriate for that. If you also have a website that gets any organic traffic, adding ChatFlow costs $39/month and works passively. Many businesses find their website converts better than social because the intent is higher — it's worth testing even if Instagram feels like your main channel today.
