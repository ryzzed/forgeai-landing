Frequently Asked Questions

How does a chatbot help concrete resurfacing companies book more jobs?
A chatbot qualifies surface type, crack width and pattern, and structural soundness — then explains how a resurfacing overlay at $3-$8 per sq ft compares to full replacement at $8-$15 per sq ft. Homeowners who understand resurfacing is a legitimate structural option, not just cosmetic paint, are ready to commit before the estimator arrives.
When can concrete be resurfaced instead of replaced?
Concrete is a good resurfacing candidate when surface cracks are hairline to 1/4 inch wide, the base is structurally sound with no heaving or significant settling, and there is no active moisture intrusion from below. Widespread alligator cracking, heaved sections, or base failure require full removal. ChatFlow determines candidacy before the estimate visit.
What decorative options are available with concrete resurfacing?
Concrete overlays can be finished as broom-texture, stamped to resemble stone or brick patterns, stained in earth tones or bold colors, or polished for a smooth modern look.
