Slow drip in a kitchen sink cabinet is usually either the supply shut-off valve (small braided line), the P-trap or drain connection, or the faucet supply connections at the top. Is the water clear or does it smell like drain water?

Clear water toward the back wall is most likely the supply shut-off valve or the compression fitting behind the cabinet -- a common failure point on homes over 15 years old. Can you see where the water pipes come out of the wall? Are they copper, silver-gray metal, or gray plastic?
Gray plastic. House was built in 1989.
1989 construction with gray plastic supply pipes is likely polybutylene -- a pipe material that was recalled and is prone to sudden failure without warning. How does a chatbot help plumbing repair companies book more service calls?
A chatbot triages leak urgency, fixture type, and pipe material — then books same-day or same-week service calls. Homeowners with an active leak will book the first plumber who answers. What are the most urgent plumbing repair scenarios?
Most urgent: active water flowing from a burst pipe, water heater failure, toilet overflow. Less urgent but time-sensitive: slow drain backups, leaky faucets, running toilets. ChatFlow triages urgency to route emergency same-day vs scheduled next-day visits appropriately.
Can the chatbot identify galvanized or polybutylene pipe issues?
Yes. ChatFlow asks about house age and visible pipe color to identify homes with aging galvanized steel, polybutylene pipe (prone to failure in homes built 1978-1995), or original cast iron drain lines — all situations requiring proactive repiping conversations rather than single-repair quotes.
