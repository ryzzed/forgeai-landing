What the chatbot handles for your window cleaning business

Every quote request captured — even when you're mid-job

Job scoping and qualification

Property type (residential/commercial), number of stories, window count, interior vs exterior only, and special conditions like hard-water staining or screen removal. You arrive at every quote prepared.

Ballpark pricing by job type

Share typical ranges: single-story exterior, two-story full service, commercial storefronts, post-construction cleans. Homeowners who know your rough price before calling are more likely to book and less likely to sticker-shock when you quote.

Service area check

Ask for zip code upfront and confirm coverage before investing in a full intake. No more driving 45 minutes to quote a job that's out of your profitable range — the bot filters that before your phone rings.

Recurring service upsell

After capturing a one-time quote request, the bot can mention your recurring maintenance plans: "Many of our clients do quarterly or bi-annual service — usually 10-15% less than one-off pricing." Plants the idea before you even show up.

Common questions

How do I handle commercial window cleaning inquiries differently?

Configure the bot to ask different qualifying questions for commercial leads: building type, square footage or number of floors, frequency needed (monthly, quarterly, or one-time), and whether they need after-hours service to avoid customer disruption. Commercial jobs are higher value but require more scoping — the bot should capture enough to give you context for a proper site visit, not quote from a chat conversation.

What should I say about hard-water staining and specialty treatments?

Be transparent: "Hard-water buildup (common in homes with sprinkler systems that hit the glass) requires a mineral deposit treatment — typically an additional $50-100 depending on severity. We assess this on-site and won't add it without telling you first." This manages expectations before you arrive and prevents the awkward "that wasn't in the quote" conversation.

I do both window cleaning and gutter cleaning — can the chatbot handle both?

Yes — configure the bot with both service lines and their respective pricing ranges. Many homeowners want both done in one visit. "We also do gutter cleaning — adding gutters to a window cleaning appointment is typically $100-150 for a standard home, and saves you scheduling two separate visits." The bot should offer this naturally when someone asks about windows.
