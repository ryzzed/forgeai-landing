Frequently Asked Questions

How does a chatbot help driveway repair companies book more jobs?
A chatbot qualifies crack severity, driveway surface type, and age — then recommends crack fill vs reseal vs replacement and creates appropriate urgency. Homeowners who understand that ignoring a 1-inch crack leads to a $5,000 replacement are far more likely to book a $400 repair this week.
What is the difference between crack filling, resurfacing, and driveway replacement?
Crack filling repairs individual cracks and costs $100-$300. Resurfacing applies a new layer over structurally sound asphalt for $1,500-$3,000. Replacement removes and repaves the full driveway for $4,000-$10,000+. ChatFlow triages which service is appropriate based on crack width, edge crumbling, and base integrity.
Can the chatbot explain when to repair vs replace a driveway?
Yes. ChatFlow asks about crack width, alligator cracking pattern, edge crumbling, and driveway age to recommend the appropriate service. Cracks under 1/2 inch are ideal for filling and sealing. Widespread alligator cracking or base failure typically requires replacement. Setting these expectations early reduces no-shows and surprise rejections on the estimate visit.
