Hi! Thinking about building an inground pool? Tell me what you're envisioning — pool type, size, any features like a spa or water feature?
We want an inground pool, probably 15x30 with a spa. Not sure if we should do gunite or fiberglass. What's the difference?
Great question — it's the most important decision in the process.

Gunite (concrete): Fully custom — any shape, size, depth, or feature. 3–5 month build time. Higher upfront cost. Requires resurfacing every 10–15 years. Maximum design flexibility.

Fiberglass shell: Factory-molded — faster install (6–10 weeks), smoother surface that resists algae, lower lifetime chemical costs. Limited to available mold shapes, though most popular sizes including 15x30 are common molds.

For a 15x30 with a spa: fiberglass is a great fit if you like one of the available designs. Gunite is the choice if you want a specific shape, freeform, or beach entry.

Do you have an aesthetic in mind — clean modern lines, tropical freeform, or something else?
We like clean modern lines. What's the full cost going to be, including everything?
Good question to ask — the "shell price" doesn't tell the full story. Here's the all-in picture for a 15x30 pool + spa:

• Fiberglass pool + spa: $65,000–90,000 complete
• Gunite pool + spa: $80,000–115,000 complete

"Complete" means pool/spa, equipment pad (pump, filter, heater, automation), concrete coping and decking around the pool, permit, backfill, and startup chemicals. Does not include fencing (required by code, $2,500–5,000), landscaping restoration, or optional upgrades like LED lighting, water features, or automation apps.
