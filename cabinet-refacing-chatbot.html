Box condition qualification

Refacing only makes sense when the cabinet boxes are structurally sound — configure ChatFlow to ask about box condition (solid and square vs any sagging, water damage, or failing joints) and whether the customer wants to change the kitchen layout. Sound boxes in a satisfactory layout = strong refacing candidate. Damaged boxes or desired layout changes = replacement conversation. Pre-qualifying this saves your estimator from trips that won't close and focuses your schedule on the right prospects.

Hardware and interior upgrade bundling

Customers spending $4,000–8,000 on refacing are highly receptive to hardware and interior upgrades — configure ChatFlow to surface these during the price conversation. New hardware pulls ($150–350), soft-close hinges and drawer glides ($200–400), and pull-out shelf organizers ($150–400 per unit) add $500–1,200 to the average job with minimal additional labor. Capturing add-on interest during the chat means your estimator arrives with the right samples and the customer has already visualized the upgrades.

Material direction capture before the estimate

Door material selection drives the largest cost difference in refacing — configure ChatFlow to ask about the customer's direction: thermofoil (uniform color, no grain, easiest to clean), wood veneer (natural grain, stainable), or painted MDF (white, gray, or custom color). Customers who arrive at the estimate having already narrowed their material direction close faster and require less sample-review time. Pre-capturing this also lets your estimator bring the right samples rather than the full catalog.
