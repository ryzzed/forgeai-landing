What is the difference between soft washing and pressure washing a roof?
Soft washing uses low-pressure water with a biodegradable algaecide to kill moss, algae, and lichen at the root without dislodging granules. Pressure washing can strip granules, void manufacturer warranties, and damage flashing. ChatFlow explains why soft wash is the professional standard — justifying the premium over DIY or pressure-only services.
How does algae and moss damage roofing shingles?
Algae feeds on the limestone filler in asphalt shingles. Moss retains moisture against the shingle surface, accelerating granule loss and causing edges to curl and lift. Lichen bonds to the shingle surface and causes granule loss when removed without correct chemical treatment.
