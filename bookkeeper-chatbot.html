Common questions

Can the chatbot handle questions about tax services vs. bookkeeping?

Yes — configure the bot to explain the distinction clearly. Bookkeeping is ongoing recordkeeping; tax preparation is annual filing. If you offer both, describe each. If you refer out for taxes, explain that and set expectations. Many small business owners don't fully understand the distinction, and a clear explanation builds trust and avoids mismatched expectations.

What about catch-up bookkeeping (books that are months or years behind)?

Catch-up work is common and can be configured in the system prompt as a service offering. The bot can explain how you approach it: "We do a lot of catch-up projects — we'll assess how far behind the books are, provide a project quote, and get you current before transitioning to monthly service." This captures prospects who are too embarrassed to call because their books are a mess.

I specialize in a specific industry — can I configure that?

Absolutely. If you specialize in restaurants, construction, real estate investors, medical practices, or any other vertical, configure that in the system prompt. Industry-specific bookkeepers command premium fees and the bot can explain why your specialization matters — saving you from generic price-shopping conversations.
