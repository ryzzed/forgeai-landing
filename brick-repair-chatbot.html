Frequently Asked Questions

How does a chatbot help brick repair companies book more jobs?
A chatbot educates homeowners on why deteriorating mortar joints are urgent — water infiltration, freeze-thaw damage, and structural risk — and converts hesitation into a booked estimate. It captures location, surface type, and visible damage description before your estimator ever visits.
What is tuckpointing and when is it needed?
Tuckpointing is the process of removing deteriorated mortar from brick joints and refilling them with fresh mortar to prevent water infiltration and structural damage. It is typically needed every 25–30 years, or sooner if mortar crumbles when touched, joints are recessed more than 1/4 inch, or white efflorescence staining appears on the brick face.
Can the chatbot identify whether damage is cosmetic or structural?
The chatbot asks targeted questions — Is the brick face spalling? Are cracks horizontal, vertical, or stair-step? Is there leaning or bowing? — to triage cosmetic tuckpointing vs structural brick repair vs chimney rebuilding. This helps your estimator prioritize urgent structural jobs and quote accurately on the first visit.
