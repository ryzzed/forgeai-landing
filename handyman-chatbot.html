Job intake and scoping

Type of work, approximate size, how many items, property type — the context you need to arrive prepared and quote accurately. Captures the full job list from homeowners with multiple tasks, so your first visit is efficient.

Ballpark estimates

Give price ranges for common tasks — ceiling fan install, drywall patch, door adjustment, TV mounting, furniture assembly. Homeowners who know your rough rate before calling are more likely to follow through and less likely to be surprised.

Service area qualification

Confirm the zip code is in your territory before capturing the lead. No more quoting jobs that are too far out of range to be profitable — the bot confirms coverage upfront and sets the expectation before you call back.

License and insurance FAQ

Are you licensed? Insured? Can you pull permits? These questions determine whether a homeowner trusts you with their home. Answer them clearly and consistently in every conversation — building confidence before the first callback.

Common questions

What jobs should I tell the chatbot I don't do?

Be explicit about your scope limits in the system prompt: "We don't do major electrical work, HVAC, roofing, or jobs requiring a specialized license beyond general contractor." When a homeowner asks about something outside your scope, the bot should say so directly and suggest they contact a specialist — not waste their time or yours on a job you won't take. Clear scope limits also protect you from liability questions about work you're not qualified to do.

How do I handle multi-item job lists without being too far off on estimates?

Configure the bot to give per-item ranges and a combined total estimate with a caveat: "This is a ballpark — the final quote depends on what I see on-site, particularly for things like drywall where the size and extent determine the time." Most homeowners understand that estimates require a site visit. The ballpark gets them engaged; the caveat manages expectations. Add "I do confirm the scope and price before starting any work" to remove commitment anxiety.

I specialize in certain types of work — how do I communicate that?

Configure your specializations prominently in the system prompt: "We specialize in kitchen and bathroom repairs, tile work, and carpentry. We're comfortable with most general repairs but particularly experienced with finish work, door and window adjustments, and bathroom fixture replacement." This attracts better-fit clients and lets the bot highlight your strengths rather than presenting you as a generalist who does anything.
