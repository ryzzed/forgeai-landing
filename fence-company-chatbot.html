Common questions

How should the chatbot handle permit questions?

Be clear about who's responsible: "Most municipalities require a permit for fences over 4 feet. We pull the permit as part of the job — typically $75-150 depending on your jurisdiction. We handle the paperwork and inspection scheduling. You should also check with your HOA if applicable, since they may have additional restrictions we'd need to work within." Pulling permits is standard for established fence companies — the bot positions it as a service feature, not a hassle.

What about underground utility locating — should the chatbot mention it?

Yes — it's a professional differentiator: "Before we set any posts, we call 811 (Call Before You Dig) to have underground utilities marked. It's required by law and protects your property and our crew. We handle this automatically as part of every installation." Many homeowners don't know this is required or done — mentioning it proactively builds confidence that you run a professional operation.

I do both residential and commercial fencing — how should the bot handle commercial inquiries?

Residential and commercial are different qualification paths: "For commercial fencing — parking lots, industrial facilities, sports facilities, perimeter security — we do a site survey and formal bid. What type of facility and roughly how many linear feet? I can get our commercial estimator in touch with you." Commercial fence work often involves security fencing, high-visibility projects, and different decision-maker processes — the bot should capture the lead and route to a different follow-up track.
