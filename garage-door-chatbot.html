Common questions

How do I handle the "how much does a spring replacement cost" question honestly?

Be transparent: "Spring replacement typically runs $200-350 for a single spring, $280-450 for two springs (we recommend replacing both even if only one broke — they wear together). The cost depends on spring type (torsion vs extension) and spring size, which varies by door weight. If the cable also needs replacing, add $75-100. We can confirm the exact type and quote firm when we arrive." This level of honesty builds trust and filters out customers who are only looking for the cheapest option, which tends to attract call-backs on botched work anyway.

Should I advertise same-day or next-day service guarantees through the chatbot?

Only if you can consistently deliver. "We offer same-day service for emergency repairs when availability allows" is honest. "Guaranteed same-day service" creates a promise you may not be able to keep during busy seasons. The chatbot should say: "We prioritize emergency calls and typically reach customers within 2-4 hours for urgent repairs. For non-emergency service, we schedule appointments within 24-48 hours." Under-promise and over-deliver beats the opposite every time.

I do both residential and commercial — should the bot handle both?

Yes, with a clear split: "For residential garage doors, we typically schedule same-day or next-day. For commercial overhead doors, rolling steel, and high-use applications, we do a site assessment and provide a commercial quote — different hardware and installation requirements. Which type of property is this?" Commercial garage door work (warehouses, loading docks, commercial buildings) often has higher margins and different customer relationships — capturing that separately helps you route it to the right estimator.
