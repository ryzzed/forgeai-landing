What the chatbot handles for your painting business

Every quote request captured — even when you're painting

Project scoping

Interior vs exterior, square footage or room count, surface condition, ceiling height, trim inclusion, and whether prep work (wallpaper removal, repairs) is needed. The context you need to quote competitively and accurately.

Ballpark estimates

Per-room rates, whole-house interior pricing, exterior by square footage, cabinet painting quotes. Ranges that are specific enough to be useful — and honest enough to set realistic expectations before the walk-through.

Paint and product standards

What brands and grades you use, whether customers can supply their own paint, how many coats are included. These details close the quality gap between you and cheaper competitors — and filter out customers who want discount work.

Timeline and scheduling

Current availability, typical project duration, whether you work weekends. Homeowners planning around moves, renovations, or real estate deadlines need this before committing to an estimate visit — the bot answers it without a callback.

Common questions

How do I handle cabinet painting inquiries specifically — it's a different specialty?

Configure a separate pricing structure: "Cabinet painting is a specialty service — we spray cabinets off-site or in-place with professional spraying equipment for a factory-smooth finish. Full kitchen cabinet repaint (doors + boxes + hardware replacement): typically $2,500-4,500 depending on cabinet count. This is different from brush-and-roll work — the prep and finish quality are significantly higher." Customers often don't understand why cabinet painting costs more; the bot can explain it before they sticker-shock when you quote.

I also do commercial work — can the chatbot handle commercial inquiries?

Yes — configure a commercial path: "For commercial projects (offices, retail, multi-unit), we do a site visit and submit a formal bid. What type of property is it, roughly how many square feet, and what's your timeline for the work?" Commercial leads have different qualification needs (scope documents, bid timelines, decision-makers) so the bot should capture enough to route to a proper commercial estimate process, not just a residential callback.

What should I say about color consultation?

If you offer it: "We include a basic color consultation with every estimate — I can pull fan decks and make recommendations based on lighting and existing finishes in your home." If you don't: "We're happy to work with whatever colors you choose — we recommend Sherwin-Williams or Benjamin Moore for the best coverage and durability. Your local store can help with color selection before we start." Either way, addressing the color question in the chat builds confidence for homeowners who are anxious about making the wrong choice.
