Common questions

Should I publish exact prices or ranges?

Ranges work better for pressure washing because pricing genuinely varies by property condition, accessibility, and add-on services. "House washing for a 1,500-2,500 sq ft home runs $200-350" is honest and filters out bargain hunters without alienating serious buyers. Adding context ("2-story homes at the higher end, single story at the lower") makes the range more useful and demonstrates that you know your business.

We also do window cleaning and gutter cleaning — can the chatbot handle all services?

Yes — configure all your services with their own pricing ranges and upsell prompts. "While we're there for house washing, many customers add gutter cleaning ($75-120) and window cleaning ($150-200) — we offer a package discount when services are combined." The bot can mention complementary services naturally during the quote conversation, increasing your average job value.

How do I handle availability questions if my schedule changes week to week?

Give the bot a general availability window ("We're currently scheduling about 2 weeks out") rather than specific dates, and update it weekly when your lead time changes. The bot can also say "availability varies — I'll include your preferred timing in the quote request so the owner can confirm the best date when they call." This sets accurate expectations without requiring real-time calendar integration.
