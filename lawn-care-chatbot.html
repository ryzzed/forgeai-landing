Fertilization and weed program bundling

A mowing customer who also signs for a fertilization and weed control program is worth 2–3x more annually than a mowing-only customer. Configure ChatFlow to ask about current lawn health — weeds, bare spots, color — as part of the intake. Customers with visible problems are highly receptive to the fert program because ChatFlow can explain the connection: mowing maintains height, but only a treatment program addresses weeds and nutrition. Aeration and overseeding upsells

Fall aeration and overseeding is the highest-value one-time service in lawn care — typically $150–350 per application. Configure ChatFlow to ask about lawn thickness and present aeration as the solution for bare spots or thin grass during fall booking conversations. Customers who understand that aeration breaks up compacted soil and overseeding fills thin areas at the optimal time of year are far more receptive than customers who only hear about it as a upsell at the door.

Neighbor and neighborhood referral capture

Lawn care has a strong geographic concentration effect — when you service one house on a street, neighbors notice the results and inquire. Configure ChatFlow to ask how the customer heard about you, and if they mention a neighbor, note that you can often route the crew to service multiple addresses on the same visit for a slight per-visit discount. Neighborhood bundling increases route efficiency and reduces per-customer service cost while growing your customer base organically.
