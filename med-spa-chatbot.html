What the chatbot handles for your med spa

Convert late-night researchers into booked consultations

Note on medical advice: Configure ChatFlow to provide general treatment information and direct specific medical or candidacy questions to your consultation. "The best way to know if you're a candidate is a complimentary assessment with our provider" is both helpful and appropriate — and it converts browsers into booked appointments.

Treatment education

Botox vs. Dysport, filler types, laser options, body contouring, skin rejuvenation — explain each treatment's purpose, process, and expected results. Help prospects arrive at consultations informed and ready to decide.

Pricing transparency

Share price ranges for each treatment to qualify leads and set expectations. Prospects who know your price range before calling are more likely to book — and less likely to cancel over sticker shock.

Downtime and aftercare FAQ

How long does swelling last? When can I exercise? What should I avoid after Botox? The practical questions that determine whether a prospect commits. Answer them up front and reduce pre-consultation friction.

Consultation capture

When a prospect is ready, capture their name, contact info, treatment interest, and availability — giving your team a complete picture before the first appointment and allowing your injector to prepare meaningfully.

Common questions

Should I include my full price menu in the chatbot?

Price ranges work well for most med spa treatments. "Botox is priced per unit, typically $13-16/unit, and most areas require 20-40 units" is honest and useful. Publishing your full price list can attract comparison shoppers, but even a range gives prospects the information they need to qualify themselves. If you offer complimentary consultations, lead with that — it often matters more than exact pricing.

Can the chatbot handle questions about specific medical conditions?

Configure the bot to decline specific medical advice and route those questions to your consultation: "That's a great question for your provider — during your complimentary consultation, we can review your medical history and give you a specific recommendation." The bot handles general education and FAQs; your licensed providers handle the clinical assessment. This approach is appropriate and converts curiosity into appointments.

We offer both medical and non-medical aesthetic services — can the chatbot handle both?

Yes — configure separate service descriptions for medical treatments (injectables, laser, body contouring) and non-medical services (facials, waxing, massage) in your system prompt. The bot identifies which category the visitor is asking about and provides appropriate information and next steps for each. Many med spas find this especially useful for routing skincare-only inquiries away from the medical consultation calendar.
