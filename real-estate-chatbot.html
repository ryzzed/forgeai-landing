FAQ

Real estate chatbot questions

Can the bot actually qualify leads?

Yes — you train it to ask the right questions: budget, timeline, location preference, pre-approval status. The bot captures this information and you can review conversation logs to see what prospects told it.

How do I keep the bot updated with new listings?

Log into the dashboard and update your bot's training data. Changes go live instantly. Most agents update weekly or when they add new listings. Tip: keep the bot focused on your process and area, not specific listings — that info changes too fast.

What if I want the bot to book showings directly?

Currently the bot directs buyers to your booking link (Calendly, Google Calendar, etc.) or tells them how to reach you. Native calendar integration is on our roadmap. The current setup still eliminates 80% of the friction.

Is this better than a contact form?

Much better. Contact forms have ~2% completion rates. A chatbot engages visitors in conversation — answering their specific question first — then naturally leads them to the next step. Higher engagement, better lead quality.

Can I use this on my Zillow or Realtor.com profile?

ChatFlow embeds on websites you control — your personal website, brokerage site, or landing pages. Third-party marketplaces don't allow custom scripts, but you can link to your site from those profiles where the bot lives.
