Common questions

What if someone claims an emergency to get faster service but it's not really urgent?

Configure the bot to treat all stated emergencies as emergencies — provide the phone number immediately and let your dispatcher triage priority once they call. The small number of non-emergencies who self-identify as urgent is far less costly than missing a genuine lockout customer. Your dispatcher can redirect non-urgent callers to the scheduling queue if needed. Speed of first contact matters more than perfect triage at the chatbot level.

Can the chatbot handle after-hours pricing questions?

Yes — configure your after-hours premium in the system prompt: "After-hours calls (9pm-7am) include a $40 after-hours service fee in addition to standard rates." Transparency about after-hours fees prevents disputes and attracts customers who understand fair pricing. Many locksmith companies find that quoting the total honestly (including the after-hours fee) actually increases conversion because it signals trustworthiness versus competitors who quote low then add fees on arrival.

We're a 24/7 operation — should the chatbot always push phone calls?

For emergencies, yes — the phone number should be the primary CTA at any hour. For non-emergency inquiries, the chatbot capturing a scheduled job is often better than a phone call since it lets the customer provide details at their own pace, gives you a written record, and preserves your technicians' time for active calls. Configure separate handling: emergency = phone number first, non-emergency = capture form first with phone as a secondary option.
