Emergency call capture around the clock

Water heater failures don't respect business hours — a tank that failed Saturday night needs a replacement Sunday morning, not Monday afternoon. Configure ChatFlow to ask whether this is an emergency immediately and route urgent calls with a flag for same-day dispatch. Emergency water heater replacement is one of the highest-urgency service calls in residential plumbing.

Age-based replacement routing

A water heater over 10 years old with any symptom — slower recovery, intermittent hot water, minor leak — is a replacement candidate. Configure ChatFlow to ask about the unit age and frame repair vs replace accordingly: "A repair on a 12-year-old unit is usually money better spent on a new unit." Customers who've been told the unit can be repaired often get a second opinion — the chatbot that explains the economics honestly and books the replacement faster wins the job. Age-based routing also reduces diagnostic trip costs by pre-qualifying replacement-ready calls.

Tankless upgrade conversion

Customers replacing a failed tank are the best audience for a tankless upgrade pitch — they're already spending money. Configure ChatFlow to mention tankless briefly after quoting the tank replacement: "Since you're replacing anyway — the tankless option is $800–1,500 more upfront but lasts 20+ years and you never run out of hot water. Worth considering while the labor cost is the same." Customers who weren't considering tankless often upgrade when the decision is presented this way — and tankless installs generate more revenue per job.

Expansion tank and code compliance capture

Many jurisdictions now require an expansion tank with new water heater installations in closed systems — a $150–250 add-on that's a code requirement, not an upsell. Configure ChatFlow to mention this so customers aren't surprised when your installer adds it to the scope. Similarly, some older homes need the gas line upgraded for a tankless unit. Pre-surfacing these requirements during the chat prevents "that wasn't quoted" friction at installation and builds trust in your thoroughness.

Common questions

How does the chatbot quote water heater installation?

You configure price ranges by unit type. 40–50 gallon gas tank: $900–1,500 installed. 50 gallon electric: $800–1,300. Tankless gas (whole-home): $2,200–3,800. Tankless electric: $1,000–2,200. Heat pump water heater: $1,800–3,200. The chatbot asks about current unit type, fuel source, and urgency, gives a range, and books same-day or scheduled installation.

How does the chatbot handle emergency no-hot-water calls?

The chatbot identifies emergencies in the first message and routes them for same-day dispatch priority. Sudden failure on an older unit (10+ years) is routed for replacement. Sudden failure on a newer unit routes for repair diagnosis. Gradual decline routes for planned replacement scheduling. Emergency context is captured and flagged so your dispatcher knows call priority without re-asking.

How does the chatbot help customers decide between tankless and tank?

Tank: lower upfront ($800–1,500), simpler, 10–12 year lifespan. Tankless: higher upfront ($2,200–3,800), endless hot water, 20+ year lifespan, 20–30% more efficient, requires adequate gas line. The chatbot asks about household size and simultaneous demand, gives a clear recommendation, and offers a brief tankless mention when customers are replacing a failed tank to capture upgrade revenue.
