Common questions

How much does exterior painting typically cost?

Exterior painting cost depends on paintable surface area (larger than living sq ft due to gable ends and overhangs), siding type, paint quality, and condition. A 2,000 sq ft home typically runs $3,500–6,500 fully painted. Peeling or heavily chalked surfaces add prep time and cost. The chatbot collects house size and siding type to give a meaningful ballpark before they even call.

How does the chatbot handle prep work questions?

Surface prep is where paint jobs succeed or fail. Configure the chatbot to explain why scraping, priming, and caulking aren't optional — they're what makes paint last 8–10 years instead of 3. It asks about current paint condition (peeling, chalking, fading) to estimate prep scope and helps customers understand the value before they receive a quote.

What add-ons can the chatbot mention?

Configure the chatbot to mention trim and shutters ($400–800), garage door painting ($150–300), front door painting or staining ($150–250), and deck or fence staining if the crew is already on-site. These are mentioned during the scoping conversation as practical options — not as pressure — which primes customers to say yes when the estimator walks the job.
