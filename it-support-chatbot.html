Support ticket triage

Device type, OS, error message, number of users affected, urgency — structured intake that gives your techs what they need before they pick up the phone. Reduces the back-and-forth that wastes 10 minutes per ticket.

Priority routing

Configure your SLA tiers — P1 (all users down), P2 (single user critical), P3 (non-urgent). The bot categorizes and routes based on impact, ensuring critical issues get immediate escalation and low-priority tickets enter the queue without paging a senior tech.

MSP prospect qualification

Company size, current IT setup, main pain points, budget range — the qualification questions that determine if a prospect is a fit for managed services. Capture discovery call requests from the businesses most likely to become long-term clients.

Service and pricing FAQ

What's included in managed services? How does helpdesk pricing work? Do you handle cybersecurity? What's your response time guarantee? Answer the questions prospects research before making vendor decisions — 24/7, without sales staff.

Common questions

Can the chatbot integrate with our ticketing system?

ChatFlow captures support request details and routes them via email notification to your team — your technician then creates the ticket in your existing system (ConnectWise, Autotask, Freshdesk, Zendesk, etc.) with the pre-collected information. Direct ticketing system API integration is not currently built-in, but the structured intake the bot collects (device, error, urgency, contact) pastes cleanly into any ticket form. Future integrations are on our roadmap.

We have an after-hours answering service — does the chatbot replace that?

For P2 and P3 issues, yes — the chatbot captures the issue details and routes them to your on-call queue without requiring a human answering service. For true P1 emergencies (all users down, ransomware, etc.), configure the bot to provide your emergency hotline number immediately rather than asking intake questions. Most IT shops configure the bot to handle intake for everything except "entire office is down" severity — where it provides the emergency number and skips the form.

Can the chatbot answer basic troubleshooting questions itself?

Yes — configure common first-line troubleshooting steps in your system prompt: "For printer issues, try restarting the print spooler service" or "For Outlook not loading, try clearing the cache." Many IT shops add a troubleshooting section that resolves 20-30% of simple issues without a ticket being created at all. This reduces ticket volume while giving users an immediate, useful response at any hour.
