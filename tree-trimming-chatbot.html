Trimming vs removal education

Customers scheduling tree removal often haven't thought about stump grinding until the day of — then it becomes a day-of negotiation. Configure ChatFlow to mention stump grinding ($75–200 per stump) as a natural add-on when customers are booking removals. Common questions

How does the chatbot quote tree trimming and pruning?

You configure price ranges by tree size. Small trees under 25 feet: $75–200 per tree. Medium trees 25–50 feet: $200–450 per tree. Large trees over 50 feet: $400–900+ per tree. The chatbot asks how many trees, approximate heights, and any specific concerns (deadwood, clearance from house, utility lines), gives a range, and books the free estimate. Multi-tree jobs typically note bundled pricing is available.

Can the chatbot handle storm damage and emergency calls?

Yes. Configure dual routing: limb on house, power line contact, or blocked access — immediate emergency phone number. Storm damage not actively threatening (fallen limb in yard, split trunk not on structure) — capture contact info and flag for next-day priority scheduling. Emergency customers who get a phone number immediately are far less likely to call a competitor.

Does the chatbot handle tree removal vs trimming questions?

Yes. Configure the chatbot to ask about the tree's condition: dead or dying trees, significant lean, extensive trunk damage, or proximity to structures typically warrant removal. Tree removal costs more ($300–2,000+ depending on size and location) and may require permits in some areas. The chatbot captures the scope and routes for the appropriate estimate type.
