Frequently Asked Questions

How does a chatbot help gutter repair companies book more jobs?
A chatbot identifies sagging sections, leaking seams, pulling fascia boards, and overflowing — then explains the foundation, basement, and soffit damage that follows unrepaired gutters. Homeowners who understand that a $200 gutter repair prevents a $4,000 basement waterproofing job are far more motivated to book quickly.
What are the most common gutter repair problems?
The most common issues are leaking seams or end caps, sagging sections from hanger failure, pulling away from rotted fascia, downspout clogs or disconnection, and hole or dent damage. ChatFlow asks targeted questions to identify which repairs are needed before the estimate visit.
When should gutters be repaired versus replaced?
Gutters with isolated leaking seams or one or two sagging sections are good repair candidates. Gutters with widespread rust, multiple failing seams, or severe pitch problems often cost more to repair than to replace with seamless aluminum gutters. ChatFlow helps homeowners understand this threshold before your estimator arrives.
