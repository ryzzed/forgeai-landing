Symptom triage reduces diagnostic truck rolls

Well pump service covers multiple failure modes with very different repair scopes — configure ChatFlow to ask symptom questions that differentiate them before dispatch. Short cycling = pressure tank (cheaper fix, parts on the truck). Complete water loss + no pump sound = likely submersible pump pull (expensive, requires pulling the drop pipe). Breaker trips = electrical/control box issue. Discolored water = bacterial or sediment issue. Technicians who arrive knowing the likely diagnosis carry the right parts and avoid unnecessary second trips, improving your daily job count.

Well depth and pump age pre-qualification

Well depth dramatically affects submersible pump replacement cost — a 100-foot well and a 400-foot well are completely different jobs. Configure ChatFlow to ask about approximate well depth if the customer knows it, and whether there are any well records or pump installation dates. Customers who know their well was drilled in 1987 and the pump has never been replaced are excellent candidates for proactive pump assessment alongside any service call — a 20–30 year old pump is on borrowed time even if it's still running.

Water quality and testing add-ons

Any service call to a well is a natural opportunity for a water quality test — configure ChatFlow to ask whether the customer has had the water tested recently and mention annual testing for bacteria, nitrates, and pH. Water testing kits ($50–150) and lab analysis provide a low-cost add-on that also protects against future liability. Customers who've never tested their well water are often surprised to learn they should — and your proactive mention positions you as a full-service well company rather than a break-fix provider.
