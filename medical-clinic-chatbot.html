Common questions

Is ChatFlow HIPAA compliant?

ChatFlow is not a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) partner. It should only be used for general patient FAQ — accepted insurance, services, hours, location — and new patient contact capture (name, phone, preferred times). Do not configure it to collect symptoms, diagnosis details, or any protected health information. For HIPAA-compliant intake, use your EHR's patient portal.

Can the chatbot handle appointment scheduling directly?

ChatFlow can capture scheduling requests and preferred times, which your front desk then books in your EHR system. For direct calendar integration (e.g., booking a slot in real time), you'd need a scheduling-specific tool. ChatFlow's strength is qualifying the patient and capturing their info so your team calls back with a confirmed slot — reducing the back-and-forth phone calls.

Will the chatbot give medical advice?

Configure your system prompt to explicitly decline symptom-specific questions and redirect to calling the clinic or seeking emergency care for urgent situations. The AI follows your system prompt reliably — if you write "never diagnose or provide medical advice, always direct to call the clinic," it will consistently do that. This is one of the first things to include in your medical clinic system prompt.
