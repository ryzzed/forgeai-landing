What actually matters for small business

Enterprise chatbot reviews obsess over CRM integrations, ticket routing, and SLA dashboards. None of that matters if you're a dentist, a restaurant owner, or a real estate agent who just wants to stop missing inquiries at 11pm.

For small businesses, the right chatbot is judged on four things:

  • Does it actually work without a human responding? Most live chat tools require someone to be online. If you're a solo operator, that's not realistic.
  • How long does setup take? If it requires a developer or a week of configuration, it won't get done.
  • What does it actually cost? Per-seat pricing punishes small teams. Flat pricing is predictable.
  • How good are the AI responses? Rule-based bots that only handle the questions you pre-programmed aren't useful. LLM-powered bots that understand context are.

Tidio — Best hybrid (AI + live chat)

Tidio Free · $29/mo · $59/mo+

Tidio combines live chat, AI chatbots, and email marketing in one platform. Their Lyro AI bot handles common questions automatically, and human agents can take over conversations. It's popular with Shopify stores because of the native integration and ecommerce features like cart recovery and product recommendations.

Pros

  • AI + live chat in one tool
  • Solid Shopify integration
  • Email marketing included
  • Human handoff when needed
  • Good free plan

Cons

  • Lyro AI costs extra ($29/mo+)
  • Per-operator pricing at scale
  • Most useful for ecommerce
  • More complex setup than ChatFlow
Best for

Ecommerce businesses on Shopify that want both AI automation and a human fallback — and are willing to pay for both features. Less ideal for service businesses.

Intercom — Best for scaling startups

Intercom From $74/mo (Essential)

Intercom is the industry-standard customer messaging platform for SaaS companies and funded startups. Their AI features (Fin AI) are genuinely impressive — trained on your help docs and support history. But it's priced and designed for companies with real support teams, not solo operators.

Pros

  • Best-in-class AI (Fin)
  • Deep product integrations
  • Sophisticated automation
  • Good help center product

Cons

  • Expensive ($74-400+/mo)
  • Overkill for most small businesses
  • Per-seat + per-resolution pricing
  • Takes days to properly configure
Best for

SaaS companies and startups with real support teams, help documentation to train the AI on, and budgets above $100/month. Not recommended for typical small businesses.

Tawk.to — Best free live chat

Tawk.to Free (live chat) · AI add-on extra

Tawk.to is the dominant free live chat tool — completely free with unlimited agents and conversations. The catch: it's live chat, not AI. Someone has to respond. They've added an AI bot called Tawk AI, but it's an add-on, not the core product. If you want free and can staff the chats, Tawk.to is hard to beat. If you want automation, it's not built for that.

Pros

  • Completely free for live chat
  • Unlimited agents and conversations
  • Decent mobile app
  • Easy widget setup

Cons

  • Requires humans to respond
  • AI is a paid add-on
  • Tawk.to branding on free plan
  • No AI quality comparable to LLMs
Best for

Businesses where someone is consistently available to respond to chats during business hours — and wants zero budget commitment. Not useful if you can't staff it.

Crisp — Best all-in-one messaging

Crisp Free · $25/mo (Pro) · $95/mo (Unlimited)

Crisp consolidates live chat, email, WhatsApp, Instagram, and Messenger into one inbox. Their AI chatbot (Crisp AI) handles basic automation. It's well-liked by small SaaS teams and agencies that communicate across multiple channels and want a single interface. The free plan is generous for a 2-person team.

Pros

  • Multi-channel (chat, email, WhatsApp)
  • Good free tier (2 agents)
  • Affordable Pro plan
  • Chatbot automation included

Cons

  • AI quality is basic (rule-based)
  • Per-agent pricing at scale
  • Best for teams, not solo operators
Best for

Small teams (2-5 people) managing conversations across multiple channels who want a unified inbox. Less useful if you need autonomous AI or a solo-operator setup.

Which one should you choose?

Choose ChatFlow if:

  • You're a solo operator or small team that can't staff live chat
  • You want AI to handle everything automatically, 24/7
  • You're a service business (dental, restaurant, salon, law firm, real estate, trades)
  • You want to be live in under 5 minutes without a developer
  • Flat pricing ($39/mo) matters more than per-seat flexibility

Choose Tidio if:

  • You run a Shopify store and want ecommerce-specific features
  • You want AI automation and the ability to jump in as a human occasionally
  • Email marketing integration in the same tool is valuable for you

Choose Tawk.to if:

  • Budget is zero and you have staff available during business hours
  • You just want basic live chat with no automation

Choose Intercom if:

  • You're a funded SaaS company with a real support team
  • You have help documentation the AI can train on
  • Budget is $100+/month and you need enterprise features
Bottom line for most small businesses: If you're a service business where one person (probably you) handles everything — pick an AI-first tool like ChatFlow that doesn't require you to be online to function. You'll actually get value out of it instead of it sitting as an offline widget.
