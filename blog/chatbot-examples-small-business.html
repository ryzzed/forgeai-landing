7 Chatbot Examples for Small Businesses (With Real Conversations)

ChatFlow Blog · 8 min read

The fastest way to understand what an AI chatbot can do for your business is to see real conversations. Here are 7 examples across common small business types — with the actual chat flow, what to configure, and why it works.

A well-configured chatbot doesn't just answer questions — it qualifies leads, captures contact information, and routes prospects toward conversion. The conversations below show exactly how that works across different industries.

1. Dental Practice — New Patient Intake

Dental practices deal with two types of website visitors: existing patients with questions, and new patients deciding whether to switch dentists. The chatbot handles both, but the high-value interaction is converting a first-time visitor into a scheduled new patient appointment.

Example conversation
Hi! Welcome to [Practice Name]. Are you an existing patient, or are you looking for a new dentist?
New patient. I haven't been to the dentist in about 2 years. Do you take Delta Dental?
We do accept Delta Dental — and we accept most major PPO plans. No judgment on the gap; we see lots of patients coming back after a few years away, and we make it comfortable. A first visit typically includes a full exam, X-rays, and cleaning (depending on your insurance coverage). Are you looking to come in soon, or is this more exploratory for now?
I'd like to come in soon. What do you have available?
We typically have new patient openings within 1-2 weeks. I can get you on the schedule — I just need your name, phone number, and whether mornings or afternoons work better for you.
Key configuration elementsAccepted insurance plans · New patient process explanation · Appointment capture (name, contact, time preference) · Non-judgmental tone for lapsed patients

The phrase "no judgment on the gap" is an example of the kind of human touch you can embed in your system prompt. Many people avoid the dentist out of embarrassment — a chatbot that addresses that directly converts more hesitant visitors.

2. Real Estate Agent — Buyer Qualification

Real estate leads range from "just curious about home prices" to "pre-approved and ready to make an offer this week." The chatbot's job is to find out which category each visitor falls into — and capture the hot leads before they click away.

Example conversation
Hi! Looking to buy, sell, or just exploring the market in [Area]?
Looking to buy. We have a budget around $550,000 and want to stay in the [Neighborhood] area.
$550K in [Neighborhood] is a solid budget — there's good inventory in that range right now, particularly 3-bed single-family homes. Are you pre-approved yet, or still in the early stages of figuring out financing?
We got pre-approved last week.
Perfect — that puts you in a strong position to move quickly when the right property comes up. Would it make sense to set up a 20-minute call with [Agent Name] to talk about what's available in that price range right now? A lot of good inventory is moving fast.
Key configuration elementsBudget and area qualification · Pre-approval status check (the key buying-readiness signal) · Urgency framing ("moving fast") · Call capture for hot leads

Asking about pre-approval status is the single most important qualification question for real estate. Pre-approved buyers are 5-10x more likely to transact within 90 days than unqualified browsers. The chatbot surfaces that signal automatically.

3. Law Firm — Initial Case Screening

Law firm websites get a mix of general legal questions and genuine prospective clients. The chatbot provides general information, screens for practice area fit, and captures contact details — without providing legal advice.

Example conversation
Hi, welcome to [Firm Name]. Are you looking for help with a specific legal matter, or do you have a general question about our practice areas?
My landlord is refusing to return my security deposit and I think they're doing it illegally. Do you handle that?
Yes — tenant rights and security deposit disputes fall within our practice areas. Most states require landlords to return deposits within 14-30 days with an itemized list of deductions, and wrongfully withheld deposits can result in 2-3x damages plus attorney fees. Can I ask what state you're in, and roughly how much is the deposit in question?
California. About $3,200.
California has strong tenant protections — landlords who wrongfully withhold deposits can owe up to 2x the deposit amount plus your actual damages. At $3,200, this is worth pursuing. The best next step is a free 15-minute consultation with one of our attorneys. Can I get your name and email to schedule that?
Key configuration elementsPractice area filter · State-specific general legal information · Damages framing (creates urgency) · Free consultation capture · Explicit disclaimer: "This is general information, not legal advice"

The consultation conversion works here because the bot frames the claim's value before asking for contact info. "At $3,200, this is worth pursuing" makes the free consultation feel valuable, not like a sales call.

4. Restaurant — Reservations and Private Dining

Restaurant chatbots have two very different audiences: walk-in diners with quick questions and event planners researching private dining. The high-value interaction is the private event inquiry — which the chatbot captures even at midnight.

Example conversation
Hi! Looking to make a reservation, or do you have questions about our menu or private dining?
Private dining — we have a retirement party for about 40 people next month.
We'd love to host that! Our private dining room seats up to 50 guests and includes full A/V, custom menu options, and a dedicated event server. For a retirement party, most groups do a seated dinner with a cocktail reception beforehand. Pricing typically starts at $65-85 per person for a prix-fixe menu. Does that general range work for the event?
Yes that sounds about right. What dates do you have available?
Our events coordinator can check availability and put together a custom proposal for your specific date. Can I get your name, email, the tentative date, and the guest count? She'll reach out within one business day.
Key configuration elementsRoom capacity and features · Pricing range (qualifies budget fit) · Dedicated events coordinator routing · Full capture: name, email, date, guest count

5. Gym / Fitness Studio — Membership Inquiry

Fitness businesses lose a huge portion of interested visitors who browse pricing pages but never call. The chatbot catches them mid-research and converts curiosity into a trial visit or free class booking.

Example conversation
Hey! Looking for information about memberships, classes, or do you want to schedule a free visit?
I'm trying to decide between you and the gym down the street. What makes you different?
Fair question! The main differences: we're a boutique studio with a 20-person class cap (so you actually get coached, not just herded), our trainers are all NASM or ACE certified, and members average 2.8 visits per week because the community keeps them coming back. We're typically $20-30 more per month than big-box gyms, but the retention and results are different. What kind of training are you looking for?
More strength training and some cardio. I've never really worked with a trainer.
Perfect for a beginner — our intro program starts with 3 personal training sessions before you join group classes, so you're not just thrown in. Would you want to come in for a free intro workout? You'd meet a trainer, try a session, and see if it feels right — no commitment.
Key configuration elementsCompetitive differentiation (addresses the comparison shopper directly) · Class cap / quality signals · Free intro offer as conversion event · Training preference qualification

6. Hair Salon — Appointment Booking

Hair salons field an enormous volume of repetitive questions — pricing, availability, stylist specialties. The chatbot answers all of it and converts those conversations into bookings or captured leads for the front desk to follow up.

Example conversation
Hi! Welcome to [Salon Name]. Looking to book an appointment, or do you have questions about our services?
How much does a balayage cost? I have long thick hair.
For long thick hair, balayage at our salon typically runs $200-280 depending on how much lightening and how many sessions are needed to reach your goal. Some clients with very dark starting points need a preliminary session. What's your current hair color and what are you going for?
Dark brown, want a warm caramel look.
Dark brown to caramel balayage is one of our most popular looks — and a single session usually achieves it beautifully. I'd estimate $220-250 for your hair length and the color goal. Want to book a consultation with one of our color specialists, or go straight to booking the service?
Key configuration elementsPricing ranges by service and hair length · Color goal clarification (helps set expectations) · Two-path conversion: consultation or direct booking · Stylist specialty routing for complex requests

7. Ecommerce Store — Order and Product Support

Ecommerce chatbots primarily handle post-purchase anxiety (where's my order?) and pre-purchase indecision (which product is right for me?). Both are high-volume, repetitive, and well-suited for automation.

Example conversation
Hi! I can help with order status, product questions, returns, or shipping. What do you need?
I'm trying to decide between the Standard and Pro versions of your product. What's the difference?
Great question. Standard is designed for casual use — 1 user, basic features, great for individuals. Pro adds multi-user support, advanced reporting, and priority customer support. Most people choose Standard to start and upgrade when they need the team features. What's your use case — personal or team?
Just me for now but might add one more person.
Start with Standard — you can upgrade to Pro at any time and the price difference is prorated. Standard at $49 gets you everything you need for solo use, and if you add a second user we make the upgrade easy. Want me to add the Standard to your cart, or do you have other questions first?
Key configuration elementsProduct comparison framework · Use-case qualification ("personal or team?") · Upgrade path reassurance (reduces upgrade anxiety) · Add-to-cart CTA or handoff to checkout

What makes these conversations work

Looking across all 7 examples, the patterns that drive conversion are consistent:

  • Ask one qualifying question early. Pre-approval status, hair color, use case — one question that segments the visitor changes the entire conversation.
  • Give pricing ranges, not deflection. "Call us for pricing" kills conversations. Ranges with context ("$220-250 for long hair with a single-session goal") qualify leads and build trust simultaneously.
  • Name the conversion event specifically. "Free intro workout," "15-minute consultation," "free site assessment" — not "get in touch." Specific offers convert.
  • Capture enough to be useful. Name + contact + key context (hair color, pre-approval, case type) means the human who follows up can have a real conversation, not start from scratch.
