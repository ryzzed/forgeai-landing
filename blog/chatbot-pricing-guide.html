How Much Does a Chatbot Cost for a Small Business? (2025)

The chatbot pricing landscape is confusing. Some tools are free. Some charge per agent. Some charge per conversation. Some charge per "resolution." This guide cuts through it: here's what small businesses actually pay, what the hidden costs are, and what to budget depending on what you want.

Three types of chatbot pricing

Chatbot tools fall into three categories, and each has a different pricing structure:

  1. Live chat tools — require human agents. Priced per agent seat. Examples: Tawk.to (free), Chatra ($17/agent), Intercom ($74/mo+).
  2. AI-first chatbots — AI handles conversations automatically. Priced by conversation volume or flat. Examples: ChatFlow ($39/mo flat), Tidio AI add-on ($29/mo+).
  3. Enterprise chatbot platforms — custom NLP, CRM integrations, enterprise support. Priced per resolution, per seat, or enterprise custom. Examples: Intercom Fin, Zendesk AI, Drift.

Most small businesses need category 1 or 2. Category 3 is overkill unless you're a funded startup or have a dedicated support team.

Free chatbot options

Several legitimate free options exist — but each comes with real limitations.

Tawk.to Free forever

Genuinely free live chat with unlimited agents and conversations. The catch: it requires humans to respond. The widget shows "offline" when no one is watching the inbox. Good if you have staff available during business hours and just need a widget.

Hidden cost: The actual cost is staff time. If you spend 30 minutes/day on chat at $30/hr, that's $300/month in labor even if the software is free.

ChatFlow Free Free — 1,000 conversations/month

ChatFlow's free plan includes 1,000 AI conversations per month with full LLM capabilities. No agent required — the AI handles everything automatically. Sufficient for most small businesses testing whether a chatbot is useful before paying for it.

Limit to watch: 1,000 conversations/month. Businesses with significant web traffic may exhaust this. Upgrade to Starter ($39/mo) if you need more.

Crisp Free Free — 2 agents

Crisp's free plan allows 2 agents with basic live chat. The AI chatbot features require the paid plan ($25/mo). Good for a 2-person team that wants live chat at no cost.

Limit to watch: No AI automation on the free plan. If the chat is offline, visitors get nothing.

Enterprise chatbot pricing: $200–$1,000+/month

Enterprise platforms are priced for companies with real support teams, large customer bases, and complex requirements. These are worth knowing about, but almost certainly not what a small business needs.

  • Intercom: $74/mo base, Fin AI at $0.99/resolution. A company resolving 1,000 tickets/month pays $74 + $990 = $1,064/month.
  • Zendesk AI: From $115/agent/month. For a 5-agent team: $575/month before AI add-ons.
  • Drift: Starts around $400/month for SMB plans. Primarily designed for B2B demand generation.
  • Salesforce Einstein Bots: Requires Salesforce CRM. Total cost typically $1,000+/month when bundled.

For a restaurant, dental practice, or law firm — none of these are appropriate. They're built for SaaS companies with 50,000+ customers and dedicated support teams.

Hidden costs to watch for

Per-resolution pricing

Some AI tools charge per "resolved" conversation rather than per conversation. Intercom's Fin AI charges $0.99 per resolution. If your AI handles 500 conversations a month with a 60% resolution rate, that's $297/month on top of your base plan. Read pricing pages carefully.

AI as a paid add-on

Tidio's Lyro AI, Tawk.to AI, and Crisp's automation features are add-ons to base live chat plans. The headline free price doesn't include AI. If AI is what you need, factor in the add-on cost before committing.

Contact or conversation limits with steep overages

Some tools offer cheap base plans but charge per conversation above the limit. $29/month for 100 AI conversations sounds reasonable until you hit 101 and the per-conversation rate kicks in. Check overage pricing, not just base pricing.

Setup and maintenance time

Complex platforms (Botpress, Landbot, enterprise tools) require significant time to configure and maintain. For a small business owner, time is money. A tool that takes a developer 20 hours to set up has a hidden $1,000+ implementation cost even if the software is cheap.

What should a small business budget?

For most small businesses, the right budget is $0–$39/month:

  • Start free. ChatFlow's free plan (1,000 conversations/month) is enough to validate that a chatbot is helping before you commit to $39/month.
  • Upgrade when you need more volume. Once you're hitting the 1,000 conversation limit, $39/month for 5,000 conversations is a clear ROI decision — you're already getting value.
  • Avoid per-seat pricing if you're a solo operator or small team without dedicated chat staff. You'll pay for seats that sit unused.
  • Avoid enterprise platforms. Intercom, Zendesk, and Drift are built for different companies. The pricing and complexity are not designed for a 2-person business.

The chatbot ROI math for most service businesses is straightforward: if a chatbot captures one additional customer per month who would otherwise have left without contacting you, the monthly value of that customer typically exceeds $39 many times over.
