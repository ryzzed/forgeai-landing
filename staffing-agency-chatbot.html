Common questions

Can the chatbot handle both job seekers and employers on the same website?

Yes — the bot's first question identifies which audience it's talking to and branches accordingly. Candidate flows focus on role preference, experience, and availability. Employer flows focus on job order details, timeline, and placement type. Both capture the relevant contact information and route to the appropriate recruiter or division. Many staffing agencies find the dual-audience capability is the primary reason they chose ChatFlow over other tools.

Should I disclose that a chatbot is handling initial screening?

Configure the bot to introduce itself naturally: "Hi, I'm the [Agency Name] digital assistant — I'll get a few details to connect you with the right recruiter." Transparency about AI assistance is standard practice and generally appreciated by candidates who prefer not to wait on hold. Many job seekers actively prefer providing their details through chat at their own pace rather than a phone call.

We place in multiple industries — can the chatbot handle all of them?

Yes — configure each industry vertical (industrial, clerical, IT, healthcare, finance) in your system prompt with typical roles, pay ranges, and requirements for each. The bot identifies the candidate or employer's area and provides industry-appropriate information.
