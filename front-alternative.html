Which one do you actually need?

They're designed for different situations

Choose Front if:

You have a team of 3+ people who need to collaborate on customer responses. Your messages come in through multiple channels (email, Instagram, WhatsApp, SMS). You need message routing, assignment, and shared visibility across the team. Front is genuinely excellent at this — it's the right tool for managing inbound communication at scale with a team.

Choose ChatFlow if:

You want to automatically answer questions from website visitors — without anyone from your team involved. You're a solo operator or small business where staffing live chat isn't realistic. You want the chatbot to capture leads and answer FAQs at 2am without touching your inbox. ChatFlow is the right tool when you want zero maintenance automation, not collaborative team inbox management.

Use both if:

You run a small team with a shared inbox (Front) and also want to capture and pre-qualify leads from your website before they become inbound emails (ChatFlow). The website chatbot handles initial inquiries automatically; qualified leads that need human follow-up land in your Front inbox as emails or form submissions. Many small businesses run both.

Common questions

Does ChatFlow integrate with Front?

Not natively — they're designed for different purposes. ChatFlow handles website visitor conversations autonomously. If a conversation needs human follow-up, the bot captures the visitor's contact information which your team can then follow up on through whatever tool they use, including Front. For businesses that want website automation feeding into a team inbox, this workflow is common.

Front has an AI assistant — how does that compare to ChatFlow?

Front's AI drafts responses for human agents to review and send — it assists human communication rather than replacing it. ChatFlow's AI sends responses autonomously without human review. Front's AI accelerates your team; ChatFlow's AI replaces the need for a team on the website chat channel specifically.

I'm a solo business owner — do I need Front at all?

Front is primarily designed for teams. As a solo operator, the shared inbox features (assignment, visibility, routing) are less relevant — you're already seeing all messages. ChatFlow is more relevant: it handles your website visitors automatically so you're not creating more inbound messages that need your attention. Your existing email inbox handles the follow-ups that require your personal response.
