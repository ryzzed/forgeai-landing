Job scoping and qualification

Tree type, approximate height, condition (dead, diseased, healthy), proximity to structures, access for equipment, and power line considerations. The context you need to prepare a competitive estimate — not just a name and address.

Ballpark estimates by job type

Small tree removal ($200-400), medium tree ($500-900), large tree ($800-1,800+), stump grinding ($75-150 per stump), crown trimming by tree size. Ranges that help homeowners understand cost before asking — and that pre-qualify them on budget.

Emergency routing

Post-storm calls and fallen trees need a phone number, not a chat intake. The bot recognizes emergency language and surfaces your emergency line immediately — then returns to standard intake for non-urgent requests.

Insurance and license FAQ

Are you insured? Do you carry liability and workers' comp? These are the first questions homeowners ask about tree work — because liability is real. Answer them clearly and consistently before the first estimate visit builds trust.

Common questions

How do I handle utility line and permit questions through the chatbot?

Be direct: "If a tree is near power lines, we coordinate with the utility before starting work — most utilities provide a buffer or require their crew to lower the line first. We handle that coordination as part of the job." On permits: "Some municipalities require permits for removing trees above a certain diameter or in protected zones. We check permit requirements for your area before quoting — we'll let you know if a permit is needed and what it costs." These answers build confidence and prevent surprise conversations on the estimate visit.

I also do lawn care — should I include that in the chatbot?

Yes, but keep the primary framing on your higher-value services. Start with tree work, then add: "We also offer seasonal lawn maintenance and cleanup if you need that handled too." The chatbot can capture both types of leads in the same conversation — someone getting a tree removed might also want their yard cleaned up afterward. Cross-selling through chat is natural when the bot is already engaged with a warm lead.

Can the chatbot explain what an ISA-certified arborist is?

Absolutely — this is a trust differentiator worth explaining. "I'm an ISA-certified arborist, which means I've passed a comprehensive exam on tree biology, diagnosis, pruning standards, and safety — and I maintain that certification through ongoing education. For homeowners, it means the person assessing your trees has formal training, not just experience with a chainsaw." Not every tree service operator has this credential, so it's worth prominently mentioning if you do.
