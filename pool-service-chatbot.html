Common questions

Should I advertise my seasonal discounts and promotions through the chatbot?

Yes — and this is one of the best uses. "We're currently running a new account special: first month free with a 6-month commitment" captures people who are comparison-shopping and might not call to ask. Seasonal promotions convert better in chat than on static website copy because the bot can mention them in context: when someone asks about pricing, the promotion becomes a natural part of the response rather than a banner they scroll past.

How do I handle repair and equipment questions — I can't diagnose issues through a chat?

For repair inquiries, the bot should gather what it can (what's not working, how old the equipment is, any visible issues) and set the expectation: "Equipment diagnosis requires an on-site look — I can't quote repairs from a description. Service call to diagnose is $X, applied toward the repair if we do the work. Want me to get a technician on your schedule?" This is honest, sets realistic expectations, and still captures the lead.

I offer both residential and commercial pool service — can the bot handle both?

Yes — start with the qualifier: "Is this for a residential pool, a community or HOA pool, or a commercial facility?" Each path has different pricing, frequency, and compliance requirements. Commercial accounts (hotels, gyms, HOAs) are higher value and require more scoping — the bot should capture contact and job details, then route to you for a proper site survey rather than trying to quote in chat.
