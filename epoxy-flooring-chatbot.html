I want to do my 2-car garage floor. I keep seeing the speckled flake floors and the swirly metallic ones — what's the difference and how much do they cost?
Great question — those are the two most popular systems and they look totally different.

Broadcast flake (the speckled look): vinyl flakes broadcast over a base coat, sealed with a clear topcoat. Extremely durable, hides imperfections well, good traction. Most popular for garages. A 2-car garage (typically 400–500 sq ft) runs $1,400–2,200 installed.

Metallic epoxy (the swirly, 3D look): metallic pigments manipulated during application create unique flowing patterns — no two floors look the same. Higher-end, more of a showroom feel. Same garage runs $2,000–4,000 depending on design complexity.

Both come with a polyaspartic topcoat for UV stability (won't yellow) and chemical resistance.

A quick question: does your current slab have any oil stains, cracks, or a previous coating on it?
There are some oil spots near where I park. No cracks that I can see. Never been coated before.
Oil spots are totally normal and fully handleable — we degrease and diamond-grind the entire surface before coating, which removes oil contamination and opens the concrete pores for a proper bond. Without that step, epoxy peels in a year; with it, it lasts 10–15 years.
