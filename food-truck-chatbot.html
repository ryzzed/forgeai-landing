Common questions

My location changes daily — how do I keep the chatbot accurate?

Update your system prompt weekly with confirmed recurring stops, and configure the bot to direct customers to your Instagram or a Google Maps link for same-day location details. For trucks with predictable weekly schedules, the system prompt handles 80% of location questions. For highly variable schedules, the bot becomes a routing tool: "Check our Instagram story for today's spot — we post our location by 9am every morning."

Can the chatbot handle pre-orders or just inquiries?

ChatFlow captures lead information and inquiries — it doesn't connect to a POS or ordering system. For catering pre-orders, it collects the event details and passes them to you. For walk-up pre-orders, you'd still need a dedicated ordering platform (Square, Toast, etc.). Where ChatFlow excels is catering lead capture: collecting date, size, location, and contact info from prospects who would otherwise just leave your website.

We operate multiple trucks — can one chatbot handle all of them?

Yes — configure each truck's schedule and service area in the system prompt, or create separate chatbots for each truck (each ChatFlow account supports multiple bots). If trucks cover different neighborhoods or days, clearly labeling each in the system prompt lets the bot route customers to the right truck. Multi-truck operators often find a single bot with "Truck 1 covers the downtown area Tuesday-Thursday, Truck 2 covers the university district Monday-Friday" is sufficient.
