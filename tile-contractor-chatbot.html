Why tile contractors use ChatFlow

Material-ready customer capture

Tile customers are often further along the buying process than other home improvement customers — they've already selected a tile or at least a direction. Configure ChatFlow to ask about tile selection early: already chosen, still deciding on material type, or completely open. Customers who've already picked a tile need only a labor quote. Capturing their selection during the chat means your estimator can accurately price the complexity before arriving — no surprises on either side.

Shower substrate preparation education

The biggest source of tile estimate surprises is shower substrate condition. Configure ChatFlow to mention upfront that older showers often have green board (not waterproof) or failing mortar beds that need replacement before new tile can be installed. Customers who know this going in aren't surprised when your estimate includes substrate work — which prevents the awkward "that wasn't in the quote" conversation and builds trust in your thoroughness.

Combined material and labor pricing

Customers searching online see labor-only quotes and get confused when actual bids include material. Configure ChatFlow to give combined material-plus-labor ranges from the start — broken into labor and tile cost components so customers understand both. This prevents the perception gap where customers compare your all-in quote to a competitor's labor-only quote and think you're higher.

Multi-room and whole-home bundling

A customer tiling a master bath often has a hall bath, laundry room, or kitchen backsplash that needs attention. Configure ChatFlow to ask about other tile projects in the home — many customers have been meaning to redo a second bathroom or replace a dated backsplash. Bundling multiple rooms into one mobilization is more efficient for your crew and reduces per-square-foot cost for the customer, increasing close rate on additional scope.

Common questions

How does the chatbot quote tile installation?

You configure price ranges by tile type and application. Standard floor tile labor: $6–10/sqft. Large format (24x24+): $10–16/sqft. Natural stone: $12–20/sqft. Shower wall tile: $15–25/sqft labor. Tile material: $1.50–15+/sqft. The chatbot gives combined material-plus-labor ranges, asks for square footage and tile type, and books the free estimate.

Can the chatbot help customers choose between porcelain, ceramic, and natural stone?

Yes. Ceramic: lower cost, best for walls and light-traffic floors. Porcelain: denser, frost-resistant, suitable for floors, outdoors, wet areas. Natural stone: premium look, requires sealing, higher material and labor cost. The chatbot explains tradeoffs and captures material direction before the estimate so your estimator arrives prepared.

How does the chatbot handle shower tile and bathroom remodel questions?

Configure the chatbot to mention shower substrate prep upfront (cement board or Schluter system, $3–6/sqft if needed), niche installation ($150–300 per niche), and heated floor mats ($300–600). These additions are scoped during the chat so your estimator arrives with realistic expectations — preventing estimate-day surprises on both sides.
