Common questions

I only take clients by referral — should I still use a chatbot?

If your practice is full and you're not accepting new clients, configure the bot accordingly: "I'm currently at capacity and not taking new clients. If you'd like to be added to my waitlist, leave your name and contact info and I'll reach out when an opening becomes available." The chatbot captures waitlist registrations and handles the "are you taking new clients?" question without you having to respond individually to every inquiry.

Can the chatbot handle intake health history questions?

The chatbot can explain your intake process and what health history information you collect before a first session. For actual health intake forms (conditions, medications, areas to avoid), direct new clients to your intake form link rather than collecting that information through chat. The bot's job is to book the appointment — the intake form collects the clinical information you need before the session.

I work out of a spa and also have my own private clients — can the chatbot serve both?

Yes — configure the bot to handle inquiries for your private practice separately from the spa. "For appointments through [Spa Name], visit their booking system at [link]. For my private practice appointments, leave your details here and I'll be in touch." This keeps the two client streams organized and ensures the bot captures private client leads without redirecting them to the spa's booking system.
