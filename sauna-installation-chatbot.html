Traditional vs infrared decision resolution

The traditional vs infrared question is the biggest decision bottleneck for sauna customers — configure ChatFlow to resolve it based on use priorities. Traditional: authentic high-heat steam experience, Finnish sauna culture, better for groups, requires 240V and more installation work. Infrared: lower temperature, direct-body heating, easier electrical requirements (some are 120V plug-in), popular for everyday wellness and recovery use. Customers who get a confident recommendation based on their priorities move faster from research to booking than those who stay in comparison mode.

Electrical pre-qualification

Electrical capacity is the most common sauna installation constraint — configure ChatFlow to ask about panel amperage and proximity to the installation site. A 200-amp panel with short run to an attached garage is simple; a 100-amp panel with a 100-foot run to a backyard location may need a sub-panel. Pre-qualifying this during the chat means your installer arrives knowing whether an electrician needs to be coordinated separately — preventing the "I didn't know I needed an electrician" friction that delays projects.

Outdoor vs indoor routing

Outdoor barrel saunas and indoor custom builds have completely different site requirements and scopes — configure ChatFlow to identify this early and route accordingly. Outdoor: site selection (level ground, drainage, distance from panel), permit check, base preparation. Indoor: room size check (minimum 4x6 for a 2-person), ventilation requirements, 240V circuit availability. Both paths lead to a site assessment, but your team arrives with the right questions and tools for the installation type.

Cold plunge and wellness room bundling

Sauna customers are wellness-oriented and frequently interested in cold plunge tubs ($3,000–8,000 installed), outdoor showers ($1,500–4,000), or a covered structure over the outdoor sauna. Configure ChatFlow to mention cold plunge and outdoor shower as natural companions to an outdoor sauna setup — the contrast therapy combination (hot sauna, cold plunge) is a trending wellness practice that customers are researching alongside the sauna. Bundling these into one project increases average ticket and simplifies the construction coordination for the customer.
