We want a built-in fire pit in our backyard patio. Not sure if we want propane or gas — what's the difference and how much does it cost?
Great question — fuel choice is the first decision and it matters for the installation scope.

Natural gas: runs a line from your house directly to the pit — no refills, ever. Adds $300–700 for the gas line run but zero ongoing cost. Best if you have a gas meter nearby.

Propane: uses a hidden tank (usually under a paver lid or in a nearby cabinet). Needs periodic refill service, but works anywhere — no gas line required.

Wood-burning: authentic crackling fire, no utility cost, but ash cleanup and some HOA/local restrictions. Harder to turn on/off conveniently.

For a built-in propane or gas fire pit set into a paver patio, you're looking at $2,800–4,800 installed. With a seating wall surround — which turns it into a real outdoor room — $5,500–9,000 depending on stone choice.
