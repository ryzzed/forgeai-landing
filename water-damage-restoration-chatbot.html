Common questions

The chatbot opens with a triage question: is this an active emergency or assessment of existing damage? Active emergencies immediately surface your 24/7 emergency line phone number with instructions to stop the water source if possible. Non-urgent assessments go through a booking flow that captures damage description, affected areas, and insurance information — so your team arrives with full context for the assessment.

Can the chatbot help customers understand the water damage restoration process?

Yes. You configure the restoration process in your system prompt: emergency mitigation (extract water, prevent spread), moisture mapping and monitoring (industrial dehumidifiers and air movers, typically 3–5 days of drying), damage assessment and documentation, then reconstruction. Most homeowners don't know the process takes days to weeks — explaining it upfront reduces surprise and positions you as the expert before the assessment call.

How does the chatbot handle insurance claim questions for water damage?

Water damage from sudden events (burst pipe, appliance failure, storm) is typically covered by homeowners insurance. Configure the chatbot to explain that you work directly with insurance companies — providing documentation, scope reports, and adjuster coordination — and that many customers pay only their deductible. Capturing whether the customer has filed a claim or needs guidance on the process helps your team arrive prepared and positions you as a helpful partner rather than just a vendor.
