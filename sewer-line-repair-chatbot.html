Frequently Asked Questions

How does an AI chatbot help a sewer line repair company get more jobs?

A chatbot triages symptoms 24/7 — distinguishing slow drains from a full sewer backup — and captures homeowners in crisis before they call a competitor at 2 AM. It explains trenchless vs open-cut repair options and typical cost ranges, building trust while booking emergency dispatch or camera inspection appointments instantly.

What symptoms should a sewer line chatbot ask about?

Ask whether multiple fixtures are backing up simultaneously, whether there is sewage smell indoors or in the yard, any gurgling from drains or toilets, wet spots or unusually green patches in the lawn, and the home age. These symptoms map directly to severity and determine whether you dispatch immediately or schedule a camera inspection.

Can the chatbot explain trenchless sewer repair to homeowners?

Yes. When homeowners panic about excavating their yard, the chatbot explains pipe bursting and cured-in-place pipe lining (CIPP) as trenchless alternatives — typically adding $2,000-$4,000 over open cut but saving $3,000-$8,000 in landscape restoration. This education increases close rates and differentiates your company from open-cut-only competitors.
