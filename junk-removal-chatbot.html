What the chatbot handles for your junk removal business

Fast answers and instant booking — matching what junk removal customers expect

Instant load estimates

Volume-based pricing by fraction of truck — the standard junk removal model. Per-item rates for single pieces (couch, mattress, appliance). Customers who know the price before calling are more likely to book without shopping around.

What you take and don't take

List accepted items (furniture, appliances, electronics, yard waste) and what you don't haul (hazmat, paint, chemicals, tires, construction debris). Saves you showing up to a job you can't complete — and the customer the wasted time.

Service area and scheduling

Zip code check, same-day vs next-day availability, and arrival window expectations. Junk removal customers want to know when you're coming — the bot sets that expectation before your dispatcher calls to confirm.

Donation and recycling info

"We donate usable furniture to local charities and recycle what we can — we don't just dump everything." This differentiator matters to environmentally conscious customers and builds trust before they've met you. Worth mentioning unprompted.

Common questions

How do I handle estate cleanouts and large commercial jobs differently?

Configure the bot to recognize large jobs and change the approach: "For full estate cleanouts or commercial cleanouts, I'd send one of our estimators to do a walk-through rather than estimating by chat. These jobs vary significantly in scope. What's your timeline, and would you like to schedule a free walk-through estimate?" Large jobs need to be seen — the bot's job is to capture the lead and set the expectation, not to quote a job you haven't seen.

What about electronics recycling — should I advertise that?

Yes, but be clear about fees if there are any. "We accept TVs, computers, and electronics for responsible recycling. TVs over 32" have a $15-25 recycling fee due to disposal costs. Everything else is included in the standard load price." Customers searching for electronics disposal specifically will be relieved to find you handle it — and the transparency on fees prevents the surprise when the crew arrives.

I offer labor-only loading (customer rents dumpster, I provide crew) — can the chatbot explain this?

Absolutely. "We also offer loading-only service if you've already rented a dumpster or container — we provide the crew to load it for you. Typically $100-150/hour for a 2-person crew, 2-hour minimum." Some customers prefer this for large cleanouts where they want control over the dumpster timeline. Offering both options in the same chat conversation captures customers who might have otherwise rented a dumpster and gone with a different labor crew.
