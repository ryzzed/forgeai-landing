Year-round use means you need a four-season sunroom — that's the important distinction.

Here's the difference:
• Three-season: single-pane or insulated glass without a full thermal envelope — not comfortable below 40°F outside. Great for spring through fall.
• Four-season: insulated Low-E glass, proper wall insulation, and connected HVAC (or a mini-split) — comfortable year-round regardless of outside temperature.

For a four-season room sized for a reading nook and seating area, a 12x16 ft room typically runs $45,000–70,000 fully finished with foundation, framing, glass walls, and HVAC connection.

Common questions

How much does a sunroom addition cost?

A prefabricated three-season sunroom runs $15,000–30,000 for a 12x16 room. A four-season sunroom with insulated glass and HVAC runs $40,000–80,000. A screen enclosure runs $8,000–18,000. The chatbot determines which type fits the customer's use case and climate before discussing cost — preventing sticker shock on the wrong product.

How does the chatbot handle the three-season vs four-season decision?

Configure the chatbot to ask how the room will be used and whether winter use is expected. Year-round use in a cold climate means four-season — insulated glass, full thermal envelope, HVAC connection. Spring-through-fall use means three-season is appropriate and significantly less expensive. Customers who make this decision during the chat conversation arrive at consultations ready to scope the right project.

What add-ons can the chatbot scope during the consultation booking?

Configure the chatbot to mention ceiling fan and lighting package, mini-split HVAC for rooms not tied to central systems, flooring upgrade (tile or LVP over concrete), window shades for glare control, and deck or patio connection. Customers who hear about finishing options during the initial conversation arrive at the design meeting already thinking about the complete room.
