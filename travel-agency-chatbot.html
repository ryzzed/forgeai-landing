Common questions

Do I charge a planning fee? How should I handle that in the chatbot?

If you charge planning fees, mention them early and frame them as a value signal: "We charge a $150 planning fee that's applied to your booking — this ensures you get a dedicated advisor, not a quote from an algorithm." Transparency about fees in the chatbot filters out clients who want free service while attracting clients who value the advisor relationship. Many agencies find that mentioning fees upfront actually improves consultation show rates.

Can the chatbot answer visa and entry requirement questions?

Configure general guidance: "US passport holders don't need a visa for Schengen countries for stays under 90 days, but ETIAS authorization will be required from 2025 onward." For complex visa situations, direct them to official government sources and note that your advisors handle the logistics as part of planning. Visa information changes frequently — update your system prompt when entry requirements change for your key destinations.

We specialize in a specific region — should the chatbot reflect that?

Absolutely — niche specialization is a strength, not a limitation. Configure the bot to lead with your specialty: "We specialize in African safari and luxury lodge experiences — if you're looking for Europe or Caribbean, we have partner agencies we can refer." Prospects who reach a specialist chatbot that knows the region deeply are far more likely to convert than one that offers generic travel advice. Niche authority converts better than breadth.
