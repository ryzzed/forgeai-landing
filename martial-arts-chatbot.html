The questions parents ask before bringing their kid to a first class

Style and program explanation

Karate, jiu-jitsu, taekwondo, judo, muay thai, mixed martial arts — explain what each involves, what the training looks like, and who it's best suited for. Help families choose confidently.

Age group and kids programs

Little ninjas (3-5), kids (6-12), teens, adults — explain the program for each age group and what to expect. Safety reassurance for parents with young children is especially important.

Belt and rank progression

How the belt system works, how long each rank takes, testing schedules and costs, what skills are required — answer the "how long until black belt?" question parents always ask.

Pricing and trial class

Monthly tuition, uniform cost, testing fees — give the full picture so families arrive informed. Capture trial class signups with name, age, and preferred schedule so confirmation is a one-step process.

Common questions

Can the chatbot handle questions about self-defense classes vs. sport martial arts?

Yes — configure the distinction between your programs clearly. Adults asking about self-defense have different motivations than those who want sport competition or fitness. The bot can identify which type of program the visitor is interested in and tailor the information accordingly, asking clarifying questions like "Are you more interested in practical self-defense, fitness and flexibility, or competitive sparring?"

What about private lessons vs. group classes?

If you offer private instruction in addition to group classes, configure both with their respective pricing, benefits, and availability. Parents or adults who want faster advancement or more individualized attention often ask about private lessons — having a clear answer ready captures a higher-value lead.

Can I configure it for an adult BJJ academy specifically?

Absolutely. Configure the specific curriculum (fundamentals class, all-levels, advanced no-gi, competition team), schedule, membership options (monthly, punch cards, unlimited), and gi requirements. BJJ academies often have distinct cultures and training philosophies — include yours so prospective students know what to expect and whether the gym is the right fit.
